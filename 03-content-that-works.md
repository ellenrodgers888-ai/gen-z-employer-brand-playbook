# Section 3 — Content That Works

## High-Performing Graduate Content

Content types that perform exceptionally well with Gen Z students:

### 1. **Day in the Life Videos** 📹
Shows what a real day looks like—authentic, relatable, and informative.

**Why it works:** Students want to know what they're actually signing up for.

**Example TikTok Script:**
```
Hook: "POV: Your first day at a fintech startup"

Scene 1 (0-3s): 
Alarm going off, coffee, commute

Scene 2 (3-8s): 
Getting to desk, meeting the team

Scene 3 (8-15s): 
First standup, learning new systems

Scene 4 (15-20s): 
Lunch with colleagues, casual vibe

Scene 5 (20-25s): 
Afternoon project work, team collaboration

CTA (25-30s): 
"Applications open soon. Link in bio."

Music: Upbeat, trending audio
```

### 2. **Honest Intern Stories** 💬
Real interns sharing genuine experiences—the good, the challenging, the surprising.

**Why it works:** Peer credibility is powerful. Other students trust other interns.

**Example Post:**
"What nobody told me about my internship:
- I made mistakes. Often.
- Nobody expects you to know everything.
- The team actually cares about your development.
- Imposter syndrome is real (but it passes).
- The coffee is worse than university."

### 3. **Career Myth-Busting** 🔍
Debunk common misconceptions about your industry or role.

**Example:**
```
Myth: "You need perfect grades to work in fintech"
Reality: We care about your problem-solving ability, 
not your GPA. Bring curiosity, not perfection.
```

### 4. **Office Culture Clips** 🏢
Quick, authentic moments of team life—wins, celebrations, humor.

**Why it works:** Shows the human side, not the corporate facade.

**Examples:**
- Team celebrating a product launch
- Colleagues joking around during a break
- Diverse teams collaborating
- Office traditions or rituals

### 5. **Application Tips & Advice** 📝
Help students succeed in their applications—genuinely helping.

**Example:**
"5 things that made my application stand out:
1. Clear, specific examples (not generic statements)
2. Showed I'd researched the company
3. Explained WHY I wanted this role
4. Asked thoughtful questions in the interview
5. Was authentically myself"

---

## Content Pillars (Theme Structure)

Organize content around 4–5 core themes:

| Pillar | Examples | Posting Frequency |
|--------|----------|-------------------|
| **Career Growth** | Skill-building, mentorship, progression paths | 2x per week |
| **Company Culture** | Team moments, events, traditions, values | 2x per week |
| **Real Stories** | Employee spotlights, intern experiences | 2x per week |
| **Industry Insights** | Market trends, career advice, myth-busting | 1x per week |
| **Behind-the-Scenes** | Day-in-the-life, office tours, team collaboration | 1x per week |

---

## Content Calendar Template

**Week 1:**
- Monday: Career tip (LinkedIn)
- Wednesday: Day-in-the-life (TikTok)
- Friday: Team spotlight (Instagram)

**Week 2:**
- Monday: Intern story (LinkedIn)
- Wednesday: Myth-busting (TikTok)
- Friday: Office culture (Instagram)

---

## What NOT to Post

❌ Generic corporate jargon
❌ Stock photos of fake smiling people
❌ Overly produced, inauthentical content
❌ Anything that contradicts your actual culture
❌ Tone-deaf or out-of-touch messaging

**Golden Rule:** If it feels corporate and inauthentic to you, it will to Gen Z too.
