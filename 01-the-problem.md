# Section 1 — The Problem

## Context

Graduate recruitment is becoming increasingly competitive. Traditional corporate messaging—generic job descriptions and formal employer branding—fails to resonate with Gen Z students and graduates.

Gen Z has fundamentally different expectations of employers, shaped by:
- Growing up on social media
- Witnessing economic uncertainty and corporate scandals
- Valuing authenticity, social impact, and work-life balance
- Preferring peer recommendations over corporate messaging

---

## The Challenge

**Companies need to:**
1. Understand where Gen Z actually discovers job opportunities
2. Communicate in ways that feel authentic, not "corporate"
3. Showcase company culture through real employee experiences
4. Build trust through transparency and honesty
5. Meet candidates where they are: on TikTok, Instagram, and LinkedIn

**The Opportunity:**
Organizations that crack the code of Gen Z recruitment gain a significant competitive advantage in talent acquisition.

---

## Why This Matters for Your Organization

Graduate recruitment programs are the foundation of your future talent pipeline. Getting Gen Z engagement right means:
- Higher application quality
- Faster time-to-hire
- Better cultural fit
- Stronger employer brand perception
- Competitive edge against Big Tech

This playbook provides a strategic framework to address these challenges.
