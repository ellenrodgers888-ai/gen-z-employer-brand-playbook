# Section 5 — Event Marketing Strategy

## University Event Strategy

At student recruitment events, the opportunity isn't just to recruit—it's to create shareable moments and authentic content.

---

## Pre-Event Strategy

### Content Prep
- Brief your team on talking points
- Prepare demo/booth setup for photos
- Plan 2–3 key stories/experiences to highlight
- Set up simple backdrop for selfies (branded but not stuffy)

### Social Seeding
- Post teaser content: "See you at [Event Name] on [Date]"
- Announce what your booth will have (giveaways, activities, demos)
- Use event hashtag in all posts

---

## During-Event Content Capture

### 1. **Student Interviews** 🎤
Quick 15–30 second interviews asking:
- "What makes a great graduate programme?"
- "What are you looking for in your first role?"
- "What would you do with a fintech internship?"

**Format:** Phone camera, natural lighting, authentic responses

**Where to post:** TikTok, Instagram Stories, LinkedIn

### 2. **Recruiter Insights** 💡
Your recruiters sharing:
- "What we actually look for in applications"
- "The one thing that makes you stand out"
- "Myth about our industry we want to bust"

**Format:** Candid, conversational (not staged)

### 3. **Event Highlights** 📸
- Team presenting on stage
- Students engaging with demos
- Networking moments (candid, not posed)
- Booth energy and activity

**Pro tip:** Phone video is often better than professional cameras for authenticity.

### 4. **Behind-the-Scenes** 🎬
- Breakfast prep
- Team getting ready
- Travel to venue
- Setup moments

**Why?** Shows the effort and human side, builds connection.

---

## Content Creation Checklist

- [ ] 3–5 student interviews captured
- [ ] 2–3 recruiter talking points filmed
- [ ] 10+ event photos taken
- [ ] 1–2 behind-the-scenes clips
- [ ] Event branded hashtag used in all posts
- [ ] Contact info captured for follow-ups

---

## Post-Event Content Strategy

### Immediate (Same Day)
- **TikTok:** Recap video (30–60 seconds of best moments)
- **Instagram Stories:** Event highlights, team photos
- **LinkedIn:** Professional recap post with key insights

**Post Example (LinkedIn):**
```
"What an amazing day at [University Event]! We spoke to 
over 100 students about careers in fintech. Key themes:

1. Gen Z wants authenticity, not corporate speak
2. They care deeply about impact and company culture
3. They're more interested in growth than salary alone

Huge thanks to the team for representing us so well. 
Highlights from the day: [link to video/photos]

Open roles available: [link]
```

### Week After
- **Blog/Article:** "5 Things We Learned at [University Name]"
- **LinkedIn:** Retrospective on student feedback
- **Email:** Summary + application link to event attendees (via QR codes collected)

### Follow-Up Content
- Use student quotes in future content
- Feature recruiter insights in training materials
- Analyze what worked (metrics, engagement)

---

## Student Follow-Up Content

Capture student emails via:
- Booth sign-up sheet
- QR code to application
- Contact form at booth

**Week after event, send:**
- Thank you video from the team
- Top 3 application tips
- "Here's what we talked about..." recap
- Direct application link

---

## Event Performance Metrics

| Metric | Measure |
|--------|---------|
| **Content Reach** | Combined impressions from posts |
| **Engagement** | Likes, shares, comments on event posts |
| **Lead Gen** | Students who signed up / followed after |
| **Application Uplift** | Applications from event attendees |
| **Content Reuse** | How many times footage appears in later posts |

---

## Pro Tips

✅ **Assign one person as "Content Lead"** - Their job is capturing, not recruiting

✅ **Use vertical video** - Portrait orientation for TikTok/Stories/Reels

✅ **Get permission** - Ask students before posting (many will love it)

✅ **Post fast** - While momentum is high, post within 24 hours

✅ **Create series** - "Day in the life at [Event]" encourages follow-ups

✅ **Tag employees** - They'll share, amplifying reach
